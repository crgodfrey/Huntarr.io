What is Huntarr?

Huntarr is a specialized utility that solves a critical limitation in your *arr setup that most people don't realize exists. While Sonarr, Radarr, and other *arr applications are excellent at grabbing new releases as they appear on RSS feeds, they don't go back and actively search for missing content in your existing library.

The Problem: Your *arr apps only monitor RSS feeds for new releases. They don't systematically search for older missing episodes, movies, or albums that you've added to your library but never downloaded.

This is where Huntarr becomes essential. It continuously scans your *arr libraries, identifies missing content, and automatically triggers searches to fill those gaps. Think of it as the missing piece that makes your media automation truly complete.

The Real Problem: Why You Need Huntarr

Here's What Most People Don't Understand About *arr Apps:

  • Sonarr/Radarr only watch RSS feeds - They grab new releases as they're posted, but they don't go back and search for older content
  • Manual searches are tedious - You'd have to manually search every missing episode/movie individually
  • Bulk searches overwhelm indexers - Searching thousands of items at once gets you rate-limited or banned
  • No systematic approach - There's no built-in way to gradually and safely complete your collection

Real-World Example: The Gap Problem

Let's say you have 5,000 TV shows in Sonarr. You might think everything is working perfectly, but here's the reality:

How Huntarr Solves This

Huntarr systematically works through your missing content in a smart, indexer-friendly way:

Huntarr's Approach:
  • Identifies gaps: Scans your entire library to find missing content
  • Batch processing: Searches for missing items in small, manageable batches
  • Rate limiting: Respects indexer limits to avoid bans
  • Continuous operation: Runs 24/7 gradually completing your collection
  • Quality upgrades: Also searches for better quality versions of existing content

Why This Matters

Without Huntarr, you're essentially running an incomplete media automation setup. You're only getting new content as it releases, but you're missing out on:

  • Completing existing series - Those missing episodes from shows you love
  • Filling movie collections - Movies you added but never downloaded
  • Quality improvements - Better versions of content you already have
  • True automation - Set it and forget it media completion
Bottom Line: If you're only using Sonarr/Radarr without Huntarr, you're missing a huge portion of available content. Huntarr is the tool that actually completes your media automation by finding and downloading all the content your *arr apps aren't actively searching for.
